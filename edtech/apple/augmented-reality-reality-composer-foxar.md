## Summary

Resources collected from the Apple Education Feature Spotlight — Augmented Reality (Wed 3 June 2026), featuring Louis Jeannin (Foxar) and ADEs demonstrating AR in teaching. Three core tools: **Reality Composer** (Apple, free on iPad), **Foxar** (interactive 3D AR model library for science and maths), and the **AR Soundscapes** project (music + spatial reasoning + digital design).

**Key classroom message:** Students are not consuming AR — they are creating it. Shift from passive viewers to active designers of immersive, interactive experiences.

## Project: AR Soundscapes (Apple Education)

**Source:** Apple Education Feature Spotlight AR — downloadable project  
**Tools:** Reality Composer + GarageBand on iPad  
**Year levels:** Upper primary (Years 5–6 most suited, adaptable)

### What Students Create

Students build a **celestial AR scene** made of 3D spheres, each assigned:
- A unique audio file (composed or imported via GarageBand)
- Orbit paths and animations
- Tap-triggered sounds

The finished scene is placed into augmented reality — students walk around and interact with it in physical space. Projects can be **embedded directly into Keynote, Pages, or Freeform** for portfolios and presentations.

### Cross-Curricular Connections

| Learning Area | Connection |
|---|---|
| **Science / Physics** | Orbit, scale, motion — explored hands-on in 3D space |
| **Music / Sound Design** | Students compose or import audio using GarageBand |
| **Digital Design** | Intentional decisions about layout, interaction, storytelling |
| **Maths** | Spatial reasoning — 3D positioning, scale, movement |
| **English / Digital Literacy** | Publishing to audience via Keynote/Pages portfolios |

### Classroom Connection — Year 6 Economics Inquiry

This project is directly relevant to [[Coaching/Year-6/Term 3 inquiry]]: Liane's Year 6 class wants more advanced digital presentations — voiceovers, animation, moving elements, beyond brochures. AR Soundscapes delivers exactly this and can be used as a **publishing format** for inquiry work (e.g. the "true cost" economics unit).

## Tool: Foxar (Louis Jeannin)

**Website:** [foxar.app](https://foxar.app)  
**Type:** Interactive 3D AR model library  
**Best for:** Science and maths — making abstract concepts visible and immersive

Foxar provides a comprehensive library of curriculum-linked 3D AR models students and teachers can explore in augmented reality. Featured at the Apple Spotlight as a way to bring science and maths concepts to life through multimodal, immersive problem solving.

**DLS use case:** Book Foxar as an incursion or trial for Year 5/6 Science inquiry — especially topics where 3D spatial understanding matters (forces, cells, geometry, astronomy).

## Tool: Reality Composer (Apple — free on iPad)

**App Store:** [apps.apple.com — Reality Composer](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/reality-composer/id1462358802)  
**Free on iPad. No extra purchase required.**

Reality Composer lets students design, build, and test AR scenes using 3D objects, behaviours, animations, and triggers. Used in:
- Frog lifecycle overlay on a Pages poster
- 3D book reports
- Historical recreations
- Immersive vocabulary activities
- AR Soundscapes project (see above)

**Design Thinking workflow** (from Dr. Nor Hafizah Adnan's AR Toolkit):
1. Define a design brief / inquiry question
2. Research the concept or subject matter
3. Design the AR experience (objects, behaviours, audience interaction)
4. Build in Reality Composer
5. Test and refine in AR space
6. Publish and present
