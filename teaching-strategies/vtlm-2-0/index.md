# VTLM 2.0 — Victorian Teaching and Learning Model 2.0

The VTLM 2.0 is the Victorian Department of Education's evidence-based framework for effective teaching and learning. It describes *how* to teach — the pedagogical structures, routines, and strategies that maximise student learning outcomes. It does not address technology integration or thinking-purpose design.

## The model at a glance

**Elements of Learning** (how students learn):
Attention, focus and regulation → Knowledge and memory → Retention and recall → Mastery and application

**Elements of Teaching** (what teachers do to support that learning):

| Element | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Planning** | Collaborative curriculum development, sequenced units, MTSS |
| **Enabling Learning** | Relationships, inclusion, classroom management, self-regulation |
| **Explicit Teaching** | Focus → Explain/Model → Scaffold → Monitor progress (I Do / We Do) |
| **Supported Application** | Vary practice, Revisit and review, Apply learning and build mastery (You Do) |

## The key gap

VTLM 2.0 is comprehensive on pedagogy but contains no guidance on technology integration. The Thinking Purpose Framework sits inside VTLM 2.0 — most powerfully in Supported Application → Apply learning and build mastery — and answers the question VTLM 2.0 doesn't ask: *what kind of thinking are students doing, and does the technology make that thinking better?*
