# VTLM 2.0 Implementation Rubric

> See `VTLM 2.0 Implementation Rubric.pdf` for the full document.

A school-leader tool for making on-balance judgements about the extent to which VTLM 2.0 has been implemented across the school. Descriptors align to the **FISO 2.0 continuum of practice** 4-point scale.

---

## The 4-point scale

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Emerging** | Some strengths that impact positively on outcomes, but important areas for development need prioritising |
| **Evolving** | Important strengths, and some areas for development that need to be prioritised |
| **Embedding** | Significant strengths, and very few areas for development that need to be prioritised |
| **Excelling** | Outstanding and system leading. Exemplifies very effective practice based on achieving excellence, equity and inclusion |

---

## Focus areas (rubric structure)

The rubric evaluates three areas:

**Planning**
Lead a whole-school curriculum approach | Develop and deliver quality curriculum materials | Enact a Multi-Tiered System of Supports

**Enabling Learning**
Inclusive learning | Cultural responsiveness | Positive Classroom Management Strategies | Enable student self-regulation and self-efficacy

**Explicit Teaching**
Focus the learning | Explicit explanation and modelling | Scaffold practice | Monitor progress

**Supported Application**
Vary practice | Revisit and review | Apply learning and build mastery

---

## How to use

1. Spend most time reflecting on the extent to which VTLM 2.0 elements are *fully and consistently embedded*
2. Identify strengths as well as gaps and inconsistencies
3. Focus on Explicit Teaching and Supported Application — what impact is teaching having on learning and wellbeing outcomes?
4. Make a best-fit judgement — don't labour over precise placement on the continuum
5. Use findings to inform strategic and AIP planning
