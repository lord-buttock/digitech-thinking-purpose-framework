# VTLM 2.0 Guide Pack

> See `VTLM 2.0 Guide Pack.pdf` for the full illustrated guide.

The Guide Pack covers all four Elements of Teaching, each with sub-strategies and practical HOW? guidance.

---

## Elements of Learning (the why)

VTLM 2.0 is grounded in how students learn:

- **Attention, focus and regulation** — learning requires engagement in a supportive environment
- **Knowledge and memory** — students process new information in working memory, connecting it to existing knowledge
- **Retention and recall** — working memory is limited; new knowledge must be consolidated into long-term memory through practice
- **Mastery and application** — consistent practice and retrieval allows students to demonstrate and transfer knowledge

---

## Element 1 — Planning

Refers to the collaborative development of whole-school teaching and learning programs that break down and sequence knowledge to be taught and assessed.

**Strategy 1: Lead a whole-school curriculum approach**
- Establish whole-school processes for curriculum development
- Build capability and expertise (PLCs, Communities of Practice, coaching)
- Develop a whole-school curriculum plan (4 interrelated layers: whole-school, curriculum area, year level, unit/lesson)
- Develop a whole-school assessment approach
- Lead ongoing curriculum improvement

**Strategy 2: Develop and deliver quality curriculum materials**
- Collaboratively develop common curriculum resources (unit and lesson plans, VLPs as model)
- Collaboratively develop common assessment resources
- Evaluate the impact of the planned curriculum
- Determine class and student needs (prior to delivery)
- Prepare to teach students in your classroom

**Strategy 3: Enact a Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS)**
- Tier 1: evidence-based whole-class practice (VTLM 2.0 is the Tier 1 foundation)
- Tier 2: targeted instruction for ~20% of students
- Tier 3: intensive intervention for ~5% of students
- Data-based decision making throughout

---

## Element 2 — Enabling Learning

Refers to the positive relationships, cultural responsiveness, classroom management, and self-regulation techniques that create a learning-focused environment.

**Strategy 1: Inclusive learning**
- Maintain high expectations for all students
- Enable full participation (reasonable adjustments, IEPs)
- Build trusting and respectful relationships
- Facilitate positive peer interactions
- Work with parents, carers and kin

**Strategy 2: Cultural responsiveness**
- Foster inclusivity and address racism and discrimination
- Reflect on values and actions to develop responsiveness
- Establish relationships and protocols for cultural inclusion
- Incorporate cultural diversity in the learning program

**Strategy 3: Positive Classroom Management Strategies (PCMS)**
Increase instructional time:
- Classroom rules and expectations
- Classroom procedures and routines
- Encouraging expected classroom behaviour
- Discouraging inappropriate behaviour

Increase classroom engagement:
- Active supervision
- Opportunities to respond (verbal, written, non-verbal)
- Activity sequence and choice
- Task difficulty (adjust to match learning needs)

**Strategy 4: Enable student self-regulation and self-efficacy**
- Teach strategies for emotional regulation
- Support student motivation and self-efficacy (challenging but achievable tasks, scaffolds, recognise effort)
- Teach and model metacognitive strategies
- Teach the planning, monitoring and evaluation cycle (goal setting → monitoring → evaluating)

## Element 3 — Explicit Teaching

Refers to evidence-based practices that manage cognitive load: activating prior knowledge, stating learning objectives, providing explicit explanations, scaffolding, and using formative assessment.

**Strategy 1: Focus the learning**
- State and explain learning objectives and success criteria in student-friendly language
- Review achievement of LOs and SC (mid-lesson and end of lesson)
- Activate prior knowledge and facilitate connections (retrieval activities before new learning)
- Use advance organisers (graphic, expository, narrative, skimming — connect prior to new)

**Strategy 2: Explicit explanation and modelling**
- Explain the material in small, concise steps (break into chunks, step by step)
- Deliver interactive and engaging explanations (frequent checks for understanding, predictable routines)
- Demonstrate and think aloud (self-questioning, narrate thought processes explicitly)
- Model with worked examples (gradually reduce as students approach mastery)
- Use examples and non-examples (define abstract concepts, pre-empt misconceptions)

**Strategy 3: Scaffold practice**
- Anticipate scaffolds to support learning (plan for common misconceptions)
- Use planned and responsive scaffolds (point-of-need scaffolds during lesson)
- Reduce or remove scaffolds as students build proficiency

Scaffolds to model and explain new learning: worked examples, exemplars, example-problem pairs, writing scaffolds, concrete materials, graphic organisers, vocabulary lists, checklists

Scaffolds to guide and monitor student practice: discussion frames, guided notes, templates, process worksheets, procedural prompts, self-review prompts, adjustments to task sequence/choice/difficulty, extending prompts

**Strategy 4: Monitor progress**
- Check for student understanding and address misconceptions (hinge questions, exit tickets, turn-and-talk, mini-whiteboards, cold calling)
- Provide specific and actionable feedback (timely, actionable, specific — clarify goals, describe progress, identify next steps)
- Move between I do, we do, you do responsively (flexible groups for targeted guidance or extension)
- Provide additional support (additional explanation, modelling, guided practice)
- Extend and challenge students (adjust difficulty, sequence, complexity)

## Element 4 — Supported Application

Refers to practices that maximise consolidation and application of learning: revisiting and reviewing knowledge, varying and spacing practice, organising knowledge, and extending students towards mastery.

**Strategy 1: Vary practice**
- Use tasks to apply knowledge in varied ways (vary context, response mode, materials)
- Use questions for engagement, explanation and elaboration (from recall to elaborative interrogation)
- Space practice (spaced intervals strengthen long-term retention)
- Alternate practice of related content (interleaving — requires students to recall and discern the right strategy)

**Strategy 2: Revisit and review**
- Identify what to revisit, why and how (schedule content for consolidation)
- Establish retrieval routines (daily, weekly, monthly reviews)
- Promote high response and thinking rates (quizzes, cold-calling, mini whiteboards, Do Nows)
- Teach revision strategies and techniques (self-quizzing, summarising, elaborative interrogation)

**Strategy 3: Apply learning and build mastery**
- Use tasks to promote deep learning: synthesising, summarising, mapping, imagining, drawing, self-testing, self-explaining, peer teaching, enacting
- Use open-ended tasks and real-world problems (connect knowledge to solve problems and generate new learning)
- Model problem solving (guide step-by-step through solution process)
- Support guided structured inquiry (*students must have sufficient prior knowledge and practise before engaging in inquiry tasks*)
- Use group work (explicit protocols, scaffold collaboration, ensure equal participation)
- Teach metacognitive strategies (goal setting, monitoring, evaluating — 4 types: comprehension, connection, strategic, reflection questions)

---

## Key notes for planning

- The elements are not a rigid lesson sequence — teachers move responsively between I do / We do / You do
- VTLM 2.0 says nothing about technology integration (confirmed absent from the full guide pack)
- Supported Application → Apply learning and build mastery is where the Thinking Purpose Framework is most relevant
