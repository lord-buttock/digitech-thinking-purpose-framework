# VTLM 2.0 Reflection Tool

> See `VTLM 2.0 Reflection Tool.pdf` for the full printable tool.

A collective team reflection tool for identifying strengths and development opportunities in aligning school-wide approaches and teaching practices to VTLM 2.0. Intended for use in PLCs, team meetings, or whole-staff professional learning.

**Not intended as** a lesson observation tool, an assessment tool, or a replacement for the VTLM 2.0 guides.

---

## How to run the activity

**What you need:**
- Time and a space for your team to meet
- One or more printed copies of the tool
- Access to the VTLM 2.0 guides (for reference during discussion)
- Green, orange and red highlighters

**Process:**
1. Work through the table together, discussing current practice for each VTLM 2.0 strategy
2. Refer to the guide pack to avoid misinterpretation
3. Colour each cell:
   - **Green** — Embedded (area of strength)
   - **Orange** — Developing (area for continued improvement)
   - **Red** — Emerging practice (area for capability building)

---

## Coverage

The tool covers all four VTLM 2.0 elements and their sub-strategies:

**Planning:** Establish whole-school processes | Build capability and expertise | Develop a whole-school curriculum plan | Develop a whole-school assessment approach | Lead ongoing curriculum improvement | Collaboratively develop common curriculum resources | Collaboratively develop common assessment resources | Evaluate the impact of the planned curriculum | Determine class and student needs | Prepare to teach students in your classroom | Enact MTSS

**Enabling Learning:** Maintain high expectations | Enable full participation | Build trusting and respectful relationships | Facilitate positive peer interactions | Work with parents, carers and kin | Cultural responsiveness strategies (4) | PCMS strategies (8: classroom rules, procedures, encouraging behaviour, discouraging behaviour, active supervision, opportunities to respond, activity sequence and choice, task difficulty) | Self-regulation strategies (4)

**Explicit Teaching:** Focus the learning (4 practices) | Explicit explanation and modelling (5 practices) | Scaffold practice (5 practices) | Monitor progress (5 practices)

**Supported Application:** Vary practice (4 practices) | Revisit and review (4 practices) | Apply learning and build mastery (6 practices)

---

## Reflection prompts (post-activity)

- Were there any surprises?
- What are our areas of strength? How can we build on them?
- What areas stand out as needing attention?
- Considering our school's priorities, student needs and teachers' current capabilities, what are our priorities?
- How can we confirm these are the right priorities? What evidence and data could we draw on?
- How could we use the VTLM 2.0 guides to support practice development in our chosen areas?
