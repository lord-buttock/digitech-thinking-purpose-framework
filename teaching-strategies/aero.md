# Explaining Learning Objectives and Success Criteria (AERO)

**Source:** [[Education/Teaching Strategies/Explain Learning Objectives (AERO).pdf]]  
**Publisher:** Australian Education Research Organisation (AERO) — edresearch.edu.au  
**Published:** February 2024  
**Series:** Teaching for How Students Learn — Practice Guides

---

## Summary

An evidence-based practice guide from AERO on how to effectively set, communicate, and use learning objectives and success criteria in classrooms. Part of AERO's "Teaching for How Students Learn" model, situated in the **Instruction phase**. Covers the "what, why, and how" of sharing learning goals with students, with emphasis on co-construction, timing, and formative use.

---

## Key Points

- **Learning objectives** describe what students will learn; **success criteria** describe what success looks like — both must be present for effective goal-setting
- Objectives should be written in student-accessible language — not copied verbatim from curriculum documents
- **Timing matters**: introducing LOs too early (before activating prior knowledge) can limit curiosity; introducing too late reduces clarity. AERO recommends a structured moment after engagement but before new content
- **Co-construction** of success criteria is more effective than presenting them pre-made — students who help define SC have greater ownership
- LOs and SC should be **returned to throughout the lesson** — not just displayed at the start and forgotten
- Evidence base draws on Hattie (visible learning), Wiliam (formative assessment), and OECD learning science

---

## Key Strategies

1. **Unpack the curriculum** — translate achievement standards into lesson-level objectives
2. **Share the objective explicitly** — state it verbally and display it
3. **Co-construct success criteria** — with students, before or during the task
4. **Check understanding of the objective** — brief questioning or think-pair-share before beginning
5. **Return to LO/SC mid-lesson** — especially after worked examples or collaborative tasks
6. **Use SC in feedback and reflection** — students self-assess against criteria at the end
