# Ngarri Instructional Model — Reflect Phase

**Source:** [[Education/Teaching Strategies/Ngarri Reflect Phase.pdf]]  
**Origin:** School-created reference card — modified from Hopkins, Craig & Knight (2017)  
**Context:** This is the school's instructional model. The Reflect phase is the final stage of a lesson sequence.

---

## Summary

A two-page school reference card for the **Reflect phase** of the Ngarri Instructional Model. Covers teacher and student responsibilities during reflection, plus a bank of 24 structured reflection activities. Modified from Hopkins, Craig & Knight (2017) for the school's context.

---

## Teacher Responsibilities (Reflect Phase)

- Prompt students to reflect on their learning against the **Learning Goal and Success Criteria**
- Use a variety of reflection strategies — not the same one every lesson
- Give students time to think before sharing
- Use student reflections as **formative assessment data** to inform the next lesson
- Avoid rushing the reflect phase — it consolidates learning

---

## Student Responsibilities (Reflect Phase)

- Honestly self-assess against the Success Criteria
- Share thinking using the sentence starters provided
- Listen to peers' reflections and compare to their own thinking
- Identify what they still need to work on

---

## 24 Reflection Activities (Grid)

The card includes a grid of 24 named reflection activities. Categories include:

**Written/individual:** 3-2-1, Glow and Grow, 2 Stars and a Wish, Exit Ticket, Learning Journal, Reflection Prompt Cards  
**Verbal/paired:** Think-Pair-Share, Quick Interview, Partner Quiz, Shoulder Partner Share  
**Movement/whole-class:** Corners, Reflection Ball, Thumbs Up/Down/Middle, Fist to Five, Emoji Movement Match, Stand on a Spectrum  
**Visual:** Traffic Light Self-Assessment, Sticky Note Sort, Rating Scale, Mind Map Reflection  
**Digital:** Padlet reflection, Flipgrid response, Google Form self-assessment, Mentimeter word cloud

---

## Notes

- The school's Ngarri model is the primary instructional framework — all coaching conversations about lesson structure reference this model
- The Reflect phase is often under-resourced in time — this card gives teachers concrete options to use it more effectively
- Pairs directly with the **Reflection Activities toolkit** (Samara Abbas) which provides sentence starters for many of the same activities
