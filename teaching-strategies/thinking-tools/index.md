# Thinking Tools — Folder Index

A curated collection of thinking frameworks, tools and protocols for building a culture of higher-order thinking in primary classrooms.

**Wiki synthesis page:** [[wiki/topic-thinking-tools]]

---

## Two Layers in This Folder

The tools here operate at two different levels. Knowing the difference helps teachers choose and use them correctly.

**Task strategies** — routines, organisers and scaffolds that structure what students do with their thinking in a specific lesson or task. These map to the Meaningful iPad Use thinking-purpose categories (Notice, Wonder and Explore; Compare, Classify and Analyse; etc.). Examples: See Think Wonder, Claim Support Question, Venn Diagram.

**Dispositional and cultural tools** — frameworks that build the conditions for good thinking across the whole classroom or school over time. These are not deployed as task strategies in a single lesson. Examples: Habits of Mind (16 dispositions), Cultures of Thinking / 8 Cultural Forces, Murdoch TAM.

When planning a lesson, choose from the task strategies layer. When designing professional learning, coaching conversations or whole-school culture work, draw on the dispositional and cultural layer.

For mapping task strategies to lesson purposes, see [[Edtech/Frameworks/Meaningful iPad Use/Thinking Strategies, Student Roles And iPad Enhancements]].

## Subfolders

### BUILDING A THINKING CULTURE/

Resources for whole-classroom and whole-school thinking culture.

| File | Description |
|---|---|
| `Thinking Tools.pdf` | Overview of Habits of Mind (Costa & Kallick), DATT (De Bono), Six Thinking Hats, and Bloom's Revised Taxonomy. Suitable as a student-facing reference or staff PD summary. |
| `Cultures of thinking in action.pdf` | Ron Ritchhart's 10 principles of Cultures of Thinking (© 2021). Core manifesto for building a thinking culture. |
| `8CulturalForces.png` | Visual diagram of the 8 cultural forces: Language, Time, Environment, Opportunities, Routines, Modelling, Interactions, Expectations. |
| `MurdochTAM.pdf` | Kath Murdoch's Teaching as Mentoring (TAM) model. Relevant to inquiry-based learning design. |
| `Understandign+Map+++Routines.jpg` | Understanding Map and thinking routines visual reference. |

**Key concept:** The 10 Cultures of Thinking principles (Ritchhart) and the 8 cultural forces provide a coaching and school improvement lens — use in PLCs, coaching conversations, and DLS professional learning design.
