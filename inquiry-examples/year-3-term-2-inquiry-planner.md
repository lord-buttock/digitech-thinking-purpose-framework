|---|---|

| **Concepts** | Sustainability, Innovation |
| **Key Vocab** | sustainability, impact, environment, innovation, future, Earth, resources |

### Guiding Questions
- How do humans impact environments, and why does this matter?
- What makes an innovation sustainable, and how can new ideas help our planet?
- How can we take action to protect our local environment now and in the future?

### Understand / Know / Do
- **Understand:** We need to look after our planet. There is no planet B.
- **Know:** What sustainability is; how humans affect the environment (positively and negatively); what impacts our actions have on Earth
- **Do:** Research, note taking, design, collaboration, participation

## Inquiry Cycle Phases

| Phase | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Tuning In** | How do humans impact environments? — See Think Wonder with pictures; mentor texts (The Lorax, A Planet Full of Plastic, etc.); sustainable vs non-sustainable product sort |
| **Finding Out** | What is harming our environments? — Simulations and hands-on exploration of human impacts at local, national and global scales |
| **Sorting Out** | What does sustainability really mean? — Students organise thinking and unpack the concept |
| **Going Further** | Innovators Wanted! — Real-world inventions; students narrow to a problem to solve |
| **Reflection & Action** | Design the Innovation That Will Change the Future! — Final project: compost system, pollinator hotel, litter campaign, water-saving poster, school space redesign, or habitat model |
| **Reflection** | Earth Guardian Journals — What am I proud of? How has my thinking changed? What is my Earth Guardian superpower? Then and Now comparison. |
