# Unified Vision — Working Compass

> Technology must earn its place in learning — and you can only tell whether it has by what thinking remains when you take it away.

This is the internal north-star for all of Phill's design, planning and building work. It is written so its core can later be extracted into portfolio, blog and leadership-facing versions. Nothing here is locked in; challenge it as the work develops.

## The realisation

Between 2025 and 2026, two frameworks were developed independently:

1. **Meaningful iPad Use** — a whole-school framework for when iPads genuinely improve primary learning ([[Meaningful iPad Use - Index]]).
2. **Position–Tension–Consequence–Challenge–Defence (PTCCD)** — a design pattern for transformative rather than substitutive AI use in learning tasks.

They turned out to be the same framework, built twice:

| Shared principle | In Meaningful iPad Use | In PTCCD |
|---|---|---|
| Thinking first, tool second | "Starts with the thinking, not the app" — purpose selector before any app | Position and Tension are designed before AI enters |
| The removal test | "Why not paper?" — every activity must answer it | "If you removed the AI output, would visible student thinking remain?" |
| The assessable trace | Required Evidence of Learning — every task leaves a trace of thinking | The Defence — the assessable artefact no AI produced |
| Named failure mode | Substitution Danger Zone | "Students used it as a form to fill out" (Planet Builder v1) |
| Application layer only | VTLM 2.0 fit — iPad work comes after explicit teaching | Caveat: does not apply to phonics, number facts, technique |

That fifth row matters: both frameworks independently scoped themselves to the **application and assessment layer after explicit teaching**. That is a principle of the unified framework, not a footnote of each.

**The thesis:** there is one framework about when technology earns its place in learning. iPads and AI are its first two worked instances. The full articulation lives in [[Framework Core]].

## Three instances, one test

1. **Meaningful iPad Use** — the device layer. Mature: nine thinking-purpose categories, planning chain, database-ready field set, principal-ready variant. Two of nine category guides written.
2. **PTCCD for AI tasks** — the AI layer. Working draft. Planet Builder v2 is its testbed: formation-point scarcity, encoded consequence rules, adversary/defence workflow.
3. **The planning system** — the same framework applied to *teacher* work. Claude must earn its place in the planning workflow the same way iPads earn theirs in a classroom: by the artefacts and time it returns, not by being used. The removal test still applies — the professional judgement (curriculum interpretation, knowledge of the class, pedagogical choices) stays human and visible; the AI carries the re-explaining, formatting and drafting that previously consumed evenings.

The third instance is what makes this one vision rather than several projects: **the framework governs how the work is done, not just what is designed for students.**

## The pedagogical substrate

Every plan — single lesson, unit, or coaching design — consults the same foundations:

- **Victorian Curriculum 2.0 extraction** — Foundation to Year 8, all learning areas and capabilities, verbatim, organised by year level (Education vault → `Curriculum/`). Always referenced, for two jobs: certainty that the relevant content descriptors are actually being taught, and differentiation — the adjacent year levels provide the below-level and above-level descriptors for adjusting tasks.
- **Ngarri instructional model** — the school's lesson structure; the Reflect phase and its activity bank (Education vault → `Teaching Strategies/Ngarri Reflect Phase`).
- **AERO practice guides** — learning objectives and success criteria done properly: student-accessible language, co-construction, returned to mid-lesson (Education vault → `Teaching Strategies/Explain Learning Objectives (AERO)`).
- **The Thinking Tools library** — Visible Thinking routines, DATT, Thinking Hats, Habits of Mind, Thinker's Keys, Cultures of Thinking (Education vault → `Teaching Strategies/Thinking Tools/index`). This is the "thinking strategy" slot in every planning chain.
- **The education skills library** — 152 evidence-anchored design skills, installed as global Claude plugins, covering curriculum design, instruction, literacy, wellbeing and AI literacy.
- **CE⁵ coaching philosophy** — Curiosity, Creativity, Empowerment, Connection, Equity.

The planning system's job is to make all of this *ambient* — present in every session without being re-explained.

## The two lenses

Every idea is weighed against two questions, in this order:

1. **Does it buy back time now?** Planning currently consumes evenings and weekends. Work that reduces recurring effort wins, even at higher one-off setup cost.
2. **Does it strengthen the portfolio?** Medium term (1–2 years): a transition toward building and designing roles — edtech product work, education content, or own products. Work that ships artefacts suits this better than live delivery.

The unifying property: **everything built under this vision serves both lenses at once.** The planning system saves time *and* becomes a case study. Planet Builder v2 improves teaching *and* proves the framework. The category guides serve Ngarri *and* seed the framework website. The portfolio is not a separate project; it is the residue of doing the work well and writing it down.

## Standing preferences

- UK English, always.
- Artefact-first: files, tools and systems that can be reused beat one-off answers.
- Apply the framework, but also challenge it — it is a working draft, not doctrine.
- Time is the scarcest resource; favour reduced recurring effort over polish.
