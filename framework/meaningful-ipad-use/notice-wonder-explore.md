# Notice, Wonder And Explore - Meaningful iPad Use V2

> Students use iPads to notice what is hard to see, hear, capture, replay or compare in the moment, then turn those observations into better questions for investigation.

This category should be used at the start of learning: inquiry launches, provocations, object investigations, science observations, local place studies, text/image stimulus work, and early questioning.

The important shift is:

> The iPad is not for recording a brainstorm.  
> The iPad is for capturing evidence students could not easily preserve, replay, magnify, hear again, compare over time, or return to later.

## At A Glance

| Year Band | Activity | What Students Create | iPad Enhancement Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| F-2 | **Sound Detectives** | A short sound gallery with voice wonderings | Worth using iPads because students can replay sounds, compare loud/soft/high/low, and record oral wonderings before writing. |
| F-2 | **Tiny Worlds Photo Hunt** | Close-up photo evidence board with spoken `I notice...` and `I wonder...` | Worth using iPads because zoom/crop lets students notice tiny details in leaves, bark, soil, insects, materials or objects. |
| 3-4 | **Mystery Material Evidence Lab** | Annotated close-up images plus short test clips | Worth using iPads because students capture texture, flexibility, water resistance or change as evidence, then annotate directly on the image/video. |
| 3-4 | **School Place Question Trail** | A photo/audio trail of questions about a real place | Worth using iPads when students capture location-based evidence, record oral questions in place, and compare different groups' evidence later. |
| 5-6 | **Slow Change / Fast Change Investigation Launch** | Time-lapse or slow-motion evidence with researchable questions | Worth using iPads because time-lapse and slow motion reveal change that is too slow or too fast to observe well live. |
| 5-6 | **Source Lens Provocation Gallery** | Evidence-linked inquiry questions from images, maps, graphs or media | Worth using iPads only if students zoom, crop, link evidence, record reasoning and revisit how their questions changed over time. |

## Example: F-2 Sound Detectives

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as observers and questioners  
**Year Level:** F-2  
**Best Tool:** Camera, Voice Memos, Clips

### Teacher Setup

Set up 3-4 safe sound stations:

- vibrating ruler
- rice shaker
- tapping different materials
- water in containers
- classroom object sound mystery

Model one example:

> I noticed the sound changed when the ruler was shorter. I wonder if a shorter ruler always makes a higher sound.

### What Students Do

1. Students visit one sound station in a small group.
2. They listen first without the iPad.
3. They record a short audio or video clip.
4. They replay the sound and say what they noticed the second time.
5. They record one group wondering using the sentence stem: `We wonder what would happen if...`
6. Groups share one clip and one wondering with the class.

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Listen and talk about sounds.
- **Digital function used:** Audio/video capture, replay, slow observation, oral explanation.
- **Enhancement type:** Replay, student voice, evidence capture.
- **Decision:** Strong. The sound disappears unless captured, and replay helps students notice details they missed live.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads because students can replay sounds several times, notice details they missed live, and preserve oral wonderings as evidence of early scientific thinking.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Science F-2:** Students observe using senses, pose questions, compare observations and communicate ideas using everyday and some scientific vocabulary.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking F-2:** Students construct and use question stems and describe thinking processes.
- **English F-2:** Students use spoken language, images and sounds to report ideas and events.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after the teacher explicitly models careful listening, safe recording and the difference between noticing and wondering. Students then apply the routine in a guided way with one clear recording task.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Modification | Mobility + Visualisation + Storytelling | Creative-Amplify |

### Busy Teacher Version

One iPad per group. One sound station. One 10-second recording. One recorded wondering.

## Example: F-2 Tiny Worlds Photo Hunt

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as observers  
**Year Level:** F-2  
**Best Tool:** Camera, Photos Markup, Freeform or Keynote

### Teacher Setup

Choose a small observation area or object set:

- leaves
- bark
- stones
- soil
- classroom materials
- seeds
- shells
- insects or evidence of living things, if safe and appropriate

Prepare a simple Freeform board or Keynote slide with:

- `Close-up photo`
- `I notice`
- `I wonder`

### What Students Do

1. Students take 2-3 close-up photos of the object or area.
2. They choose the clearest photo.
3. They zoom or crop to show one tiny detail.
4. They add a simple mark, circle or arrow to the detail.
5. They orally record: `I notice...`
6. They orally record: `I wonder...`

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Draw the object and talk about what they see.
- **Digital function used:** Close-up photography, zoom/crop, annotation, oral recording.
- **Enhancement type:** Close observation, evidence capture, student voice.
- **Decision:** Keep if students use zoom/crop and oral explanation. Redesign if they only take a decorative photo.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads because zooming and cropping help young students notice tiny details they may miss with normal looking, and audio recording captures their thinking before writing fluency catches up.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Science F-2:** Students make and compare observations about the world around them and record observations using digital tools as appropriate.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking F-2:** Students use thinking vocabulary and question stems to describe what they notice and wonder.
- **English F-2:** Students use images, sound and spoken language to report information.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after the teacher models how to take a useful close-up photo and how to use observation language such as `smooth`, `rough`, `tiny`, `curved`, `pattern`, `same` and `different`.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Augmentation to Modification | Mobility + Visualisation | Creative-Amplify |

### Busy Teacher Version

Teacher provides 6 close-up photos. Groups choose one, circle a detail and record one `I notice` and one `I wonder`.

## Example: 3-4 Mystery Material Evidence Lab

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as observers and early investigators  
**Year Level:** 3-4  
**Best Tool:** Camera, Photos Markup, Keynote

### Teacher Setup

Give each group an object made from mixed materials:

- shoe
- pencil case
- drink bottle
- sports ball
- lunchbox
- backpack
- classroom chair

Prepare a Keynote slide or Pages organiser with:

- `Close-up evidence`
- `Material property words`
- `Test clip`
- `Our wondering`

### What Students Do

1. Students photograph the object close up.
2. They crop the image to focus on one material detail, such as stitching, texture, join, layer or surface.
3. They annotate the photo with property words such as `rough`, `smooth`, `flexible`, `hard`, `soft`, `waterproof`, `shiny`, `absorbent`.
4. They record a 5-second test clip: bending, tapping, stretching, water drop, texture rub or gentle compression.
5. They add one question: `Why is this part made from ___ but this part is made from ___?`
6. Groups compare evidence and identify which questions are worth investigating further.

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Draw and label the object.
- **Digital function used:** Close-up photography, video evidence, annotation, replay.
- **Enhancement type:** Close observation, evidence capture, multimodal explanation.
- **Decision:** Strong if students capture actual evidence; weak if they only make a neat labelled poster.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads because students can capture close-up material evidence and short test clips, then use those images and videos to justify their questions about properties and purpose.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Science 3-4:** Students explore properties of materials and how those properties influence use.
- **Science Inquiry 3-4:** Students make observations, record information and identify patterns or relationships.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking 3-4:** Students construct open and closed questions for different purposes and use evidence to support reasoning.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after explicit teaching of material property vocabulary. The iPad is used during supported application so students can collect evidence and use it to ask more precise questions.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Modification | Mobility + Visualisation + Storytelling | Creative-Amplify |

### Busy Teacher Version

One object per group. One close-up photo. Three property labels. One 5-second test clip. One `Why is this made from...?` question.

## Example: 3-4 School Place Question Trail

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as observers, researchers and collaborators  
**Year Level:** 3-4  
**Best Tool:** Camera, Voice Memos, Keynote, Freeform or Canva

### Teacher Setup

Choose a small area students can safely investigate:

- school garden
- playground
- oval edge
- recycling area
- water taps
- shaded and unshaded spaces
- local walking route

Prepare a Keynote slide, Freeform board or Canva whiteboard with:

- `Photo evidence`
- `Where was it?`
- `What did we notice?`
- `What do we wonder?`
- `Could this become an inquiry question?`

### What Students Do

1. Groups walk through the chosen place with one iPad.
2. They take 3-4 photos of things that make them curious.
3. At the location, they record a short oral question while looking at the evidence.
4. Back in class, they choose the two strongest photo/audio pairs.
5. They add each pair to the organiser.
6. They turn one wondering into a possible inquiry question.
7. The class compares questions and chooses which ones are worth investigating.

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Walk, discuss, write questions on paper.
- **Digital function used:** Location-based photo evidence, audio recording in place, class gallery, revisiting evidence.
- **Enhancement type:** Evidence capture, mobility, student voice, collaboration and comparison.
- **Decision:** Keep only when the photo/audio evidence is used later. Optional if students simply walk around and type questions.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads when students capture the actual place that prompted their question, record their thinking while the evidence is in front of them, and return to the image later to decide whether the question is researchable.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Humanities 3-4:** Students investigate features of places and ask questions about local environments and communities.
- **Science 3-4:** Students pose questions, observe patterns and relationships and record observations.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking 3-4:** Students construct open and closed questions for different purposes.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after the teacher models how to turn a noticing into a researchable question. The digital trail becomes the evidence base for a later inquiry decision.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Modification | Mobility + Social + Visualisation | Creative-Amplify |

### Busy Teacher Version

Teacher takes 8 photos before the lesson. Groups choose 2 photos, add one noticing, record one question and explain which question is most researchable.

## Example: 5-6 Slow Change / Fast Change Investigation Launch

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as observers and researchers  
**Year Level:** 5-6  
**Best Tool:** Camera time-lapse, slow motion, Keynote or Clips

### Teacher Setup

Choose a phenomenon where the iPad changes what students can observe:

- shadow movement
- seed growth
- evaporation
- erosion tray
- bouncing ball
- vibrating ruler
- light reflection
- dissolving
- mould growth, if handled safely and ethically

Prepare a simple organiser with:

- `Recorded evidence`
- `What we noticed after replay`
- `What changed?`
- `What relationship might be worth testing?`
- `Our researchable question`

### What Students Do

1. Students observe the phenomenon live first.
2. They record it using time-lapse or slow motion.
3. They replay the recording and identify what they noticed the second time.
4. They annotate a screenshot or still frame.
5. They create three questions:
   - one observation question
   - one relationship question
   - one investigation question
6. They choose the strongest investigation question and explain why it could be tested.

Example:

> How does the angle of the light change the length of the shadow?

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Observe once and write questions.
- **Digital function used:** Time-lapse or slow motion, replay, annotation, evidence-linked questioning.
- **Enhancement type:** Change over time, replay, researchable questioning.
- **Decision:** Very strong, because the iPad changes what students can observe.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads because students can see change over time or movement in detail, then base their questions on recorded evidence rather than memory.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Science 5-6:** Students investigate observable phenomena, analyse patterns and relationships, generate and record data, and pose questions for further investigation.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking 5-6:** Students construct questions to focus or expand thinking and use evidence to challenge preconceptions.
- **English 5-6:** Students create spoken, written or multimodal texts using evidence and topic-specific vocabulary.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after the teacher explicitly teaches the phenomenon vocabulary and models the difference between an observation question and a testable relationship question.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Redefinition | Mobility + Visualisation + Feedback loops | Creative-Transform |

### Busy Teacher Version

Teacher records one time-lapse or slow-motion clip. Groups replay it twice, annotate one screenshot and write one testable question.

## Example: 5-6 Source Lens Provocation Gallery

**Thinking Category:** Notice, Wonder And Explore  
**Student Role:** Students as researchers and questioners  
**Year Level:** 5-6  
**Best Tool:** Keynote, Canva, Freeform or Pages

### Teacher Setup

Prepare a small source gallery connected to the unit:

- historical photos
- maps
- graphs
- newspaper headlines
- invention images
- before/after images
- short quotes
- artefact photos
- screenshots from reliable sources

Use a Keynote deck, Canva whiteboard, Freeform board or Pages template with:

- `Source`
- `Cropped evidence`
- `What we notice`
- `What this makes us wonder`
- `Evidence link`
- `How our question changed`

### What Students Do

1. Students inspect 4-6 sources in pairs or groups.
2. They choose one source that creates genuine curiosity.
3. They zoom in or crop a specific piece of evidence.
4. They record a 20-second explanation: `This detail made us wonder because...`
5. They write one closed question, one open question and one researchable question.
6. They revise the researchable question after viewing another group's evidence.
7. They save both versions so the teacher can see how the question improved.

### iPad Enhancement Test

- **Traditional version:** Sort questions on sticky notes or poster paper.
- **Digital function used:** Zoom/crop, source evidence, audio reasoning, revision, class gallery.
- **Enhancement type:** Evidence-linked questioning, revision history, collaboration and comparison.
- **Decision:** Conditional. Strong only if questions remain linked to evidence, audio reasoning and revision. Weak if students are just typing questions into boxes.

**iPad enhancement:** This task is worth using iPads only when students use the source itself as evidence: zooming into a detail, recording why it matters, revising their question after comparison, and preserving the before/after question for assessment.

### Curriculum Fit

- **Humanities 5-6:** Students develop questions to investigate historical, civic, geographical or contemporary issues and use sources as evidence.
- **Critical and Creative Thinking 5-6:** Students construct questions to focus or expand thinking and evaluate the strength of evidence.
- **English 5-6:** Students interpret multimodal texts and explain how still images, moving images and sound shape meaning or point of view.

### VTLM 2.0 Fit

Best used after explicit teaching of closed, open and researchable questions, and after the teacher models how evidence can change or improve a question.

### Framework Fit

| SAMR | EdTech Quintet | PICRAT |
|---|---|---|
| Modification, possibly Redefinition if revisited across the unit | Visualisation + Social + Feedback loops | Creative-Amplify |

### Busy Teacher Version

Use one Keynote slide with 4 sources. Each group crops one detail, records one 20-second explanation and improves one researchable question.

## Comparison With Version 1

| Area | Version 1 | Version 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Overall quality | Useful and well structured | Stronger and more defensible |
| Main issue | Some activities could still be done with paper | Activities begin with what only the iPad can add |
| iPad role | Capture, organise, compare | Reveal, replay, magnify, preserve, revisit |
| Teacher clarity | Mostly clear | More concrete and easier to justify |
| Risk of substitution | Medium | Lower |
| Best improvement | Curriculum links and routines | Explicit `why not paper?` test |

## Feedback On Version 2

The second version is stronger than the first. It has a clearer spine: each task must use a digital affordance that paper cannot easily provide, such as replay, slow motion, time-lapse, zoom, audio capture, annotation, or revisiting evidence later.

It still needs fine-tuning before becoming the final version. The next improvement would be to make every example follow the same activity-card structure:

- Teacher setup
- What students do
- Traditional version
- iPad enhancement test
- Curriculum fit
- SAMR / EdTech Quintet / PICRAT
- Busy teacher version

The strongest examples are **Sound Detectives**, **Mystery Material Evidence Lab**, and **Slow Change / Fast Change Investigation Launch**.

The weakest example is still **Source Lens Provocation Gallery**, unless the task is very strict that students must link questions to cropped evidence, source details, audio reasoning and later revision. Otherwise it slips back into `digital sticky notes`.
