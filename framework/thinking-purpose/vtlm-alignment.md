# Thinking Purpose Framework — VTLM 2.0 Alignment

The Thinking Purpose Framework and VTLM 2.0 operate at different levels and nest together. VTLM 2.0 describes *how* to teach. The Thinking Purpose Framework describes *what kind of thinking* to design for and *whether technology genuinely enhances it*.

---

## The confirmed gap

VTLM 2.0 is comprehensive on pedagogy but contains no guidance on technology integration — confirmed absent from the full 15-page guide pack. The 2027 Digital Learning Policy requires schools to address this. The Thinking Purpose Framework is the answer to the question VTLM 2.0 leaves open: *when and how does digital technology genuinely enhance the thinking students are doing?*

---

## How the frameworks align

### Planning
**VTLM 2.0:** Collaborative curriculum development, sequencing, MTSS.

**Thinking Purpose Framework role:** This is where the framework lives at the school level — pacing guide, unit structure, the decision to embed thinking-purpose categories in planning templates. Technology integration policy sits here.

---

### Enabling Learning
**VTLM 2.0:** Inclusive learning, cultural responsiveness, PCMS, self-regulation and metacognition.

**Thinking Purpose Framework role:** This is the dispositional layer — Habits of Mind, Cultures of Thinking, self-regulation strategies. These are the conditions under which students can do the thinking the 9 categories require. Not task strategies; foundational enablers.

→ Maps to the "dispositional and cultural tools" layer in [[Education/Teaching Strategies/Thinking Tools/index]]

---

### Explicit Teaching
**VTLM 2.0:** Focus the learning → Explicit explanation and modelling → Scaffold practice → Monitor progress (I Do / We Do)

| VTLM 2.0 strategy | Thinking Purpose Framework role |
|---|---|
| **Focus the learning** | Teacher names the thinking purpose for students in student-friendly language alongside the learning objective |
| **Explicit explanation and modelling** | Teacher models the thinking category — e.g. "I'm going to show you how I See, Think, Wonder about this image" |
| **Scaffold practice** | This is where thinking routines are explicitly introduced as scaffold tools — graphic organisers, discussion frames, sentence frames |
| **Monitor progress** | Formative evidence informs which thinking category students are ready to apply independently in Supported Application |

---

### Supported Application — three strategies

Supported Application has three distinct strategies, each with a different relationship to the Thinking Purpose Framework.

#### Strategy 1: Vary practice
Providing the same knowledge through different task types, contexts, spaced intervals, and interleaving.

**Thinking Purpose Framework role:** Varying the task type *is* varying the thinking-purpose category across lessons. A unit on place value might use Notice/Wonder/Explore in one lesson, Compare/Classify/Analyse in the next, then Investigate/Connect/Explain. This gives teachers a principled reason to vary — not just "do something different."

Technology: lower stakes here — paper, whiteboard, and manipulative-based variation is often appropriate.

---

#### Strategy 2: Revisit and review
Spaced retrieval, Do Nows, daily/weekly/monthly review routines.

**Thinking Purpose Framework role:** Retrieval practice is its own cognitive function — it doesn't map to a specific thinking category. But the 9 thinking categories work well *as* retrieval vehicles. "I Used To Think, Now I Think" is both a retrieval routine and a Reflect/Evaluate/Improve task. Claim, Support, Question retrieves while developing Investigate/Connect/Explain thinking.

Technology note: audio recording of retrieval responses is a genuine enhancement — replayable evidence of recalled reasoning. Applies the Removal Test.

---

#### Strategy 3: Apply learning and build mastery ← main zone for the framework

This is where the Thinking Purpose Framework does its most important work.

VTLM 2.0 lists task types but provides no language for the *kind of thinking* involved:

| VTLM 2.0 task type | Thinking Purpose Category |
|---|---|
| Synthesising, summarising | Organise, Summarise And Show Understanding |
| Imagining, drawing | Imagine, Design And Improve |
| Self-explaining, peer teaching | Investigate, Connect And Explain |
| Mapping | Compare, Classify And Analyse |
| Guided structured inquiry | Notice, Wonder And Explore |
| Metacognition, reflection | Reflect, Evaluate And Improve |

**Critical VTLM 2.0 condition:** "Ensure students have sufficient prior knowledge and practise before engaging in inquiry tasks." The Explicit Teaching phase must come first — students cannot do deep thinking about content they have not yet learned.

Technology: strongest enhancement zone. Camera, audio explanation, explainer video, annotated artefact, before/after portfolio. The [[Edtech/Frameworks/Vision/Framework Core|Removal Test]] applies most forcefully to these tasks.

---

## The decision sequence with VTLM 2.0 context

1. Where are we in the unit? (Unit phase narrows likely thinking categories)
2. What is this lesson's objective? (Given by the pacing guide)
3. Which VTLM 2.0 element does this lesson sit in? (Explicit Teaching or Supported Application)
4. What kind of thinking does the objective require? (9 thinking-purpose categories)
5. What student role does that create? (Observer, Explainer, Designer, Analyst, etc.)
6. Which thinking strategy supports it? (VTR routine, DATT tool, etc.)
7. Would technology genuinely enhance this thinking — or is physical/paper better? (Removal Test)
8. What artefact shows the thinking, not just the answer?

---

## In one sentence

VTLM 2.0 tells teachers to use tasks that "promote deep learning" and "guided structured inquiry" — the Thinking Purpose Framework tells them *which kind of thinking that is, what role students play, which strategies support it, and whether technology earns its place.*
