# Rainbow Editing Wall Posters

**Source:** [[Education/Writing/Rainbow Editing Wall Posters.pdf]]  
**Format:** 6 A4 display posters, one per colour  
**Use:** Print and display as a wall reference; students use coloured pencils/markers to self-edit their writing drafts

---

## Summary

A colour-coded self-editing strategy for student writing. Students work through each colour in sequence, editing their own draft for a specific writing convention. Each poster has a clear instruction and a visual example. Designed as a classroom wall display and a structured student revision routine.

---

## The 6 Colours

| Colour | Focus | Student Task |
|---|---|---|
| **Red** | Paragraphing | Put a red arrow at the beginning of each new paragraph. If no paragraphs exist, mark where they should go. |
| **Orange** | Capital letters | Underline each correct capital letter. Cross through any that shouldn't be capitalised. |
| **Yellow** | Punctuation | Circle all punctuation marks (full stops, question marks, commas, exclamation marks, talking marks, etc.) |
| **Green** | Transition words & conjunctions | Circle transition words and conjunctions — check for variety (First, Next, Then, Finally, And, But, Or, So, Yet, For, Nor, After) |
| **Blue** | Spelling | Circle any words you think are misspelled. Check using dictionary, iPad, or word wall. |
| **Purple** | Word choice / "juicy" words | Circle interesting/vivid words. Replace overused words (e.g. "said" → whispered, yelled, screamed, asked) |

---

## How to Use

1. Students complete a writing draft first
2. Work through each colour in order (Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Purple)
3. Use a coloured pencil or marker for each pass
4. Revisit and fix issues identified at each stage before moving to the next colour
5. Can be done independently, with a partner, or as a structured editing session
