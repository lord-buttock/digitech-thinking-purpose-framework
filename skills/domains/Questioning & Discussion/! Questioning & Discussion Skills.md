# Questioning & Discussion

Designs and facilitates high-quality classroom talk: Socratic questioning, dialogic teaching, discussion protocols, hinge questions.

## Skills in this domain (5)

| Skill | What it does |
|---|---|
| [[Dialogic Teaching Move Generator]] | Generate follow-up teaching moves that extend student thinking after a specific classroom response. Use when a student says something worth exploring and the teacher wants to deepen the dialogue. |
| [[Discussion Protocol Selector]] | Select and configure a structured discussion protocol matched to the purpose, topic, and group readiness. Use when planning classroom discussions, Socratic seminars, or structured debate. |
| [[Hinge Question Designer]] | Design a diagnostic hinge question that reveals whether students understand enough to move on. Use when planning key checkpoints mid-lesson during explicit or direct instruction. For a full protocol of checks across a whole lesson, use checking-for-understanding-protocol-designer. |
| [[Perspective Taking Designer]] | Design structured perspective-taking activities with anti-projection guardrails, developing genuine understanding of complexity rather than performed empathy. Use when planning activities where students step into others' viewpoints in history, social sciences, or literature. |
| [[Socratic Questioning Sequence Generator]] | Generate a Socratic questioning sequence that develops conceptual understanding through guided inquiry. Use when facilitating philosophical discussions, concept exploration, or critical examination. |
