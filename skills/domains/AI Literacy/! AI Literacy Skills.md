# AI Literacy

Builds students' and teachers' capacity to interrogate, critically evaluate, and productively use AI in learning contexts.

## Skills in this domain (7)

| Skill | What it does |
|---|---|
| [[AI Expertise Interrogation Designer]] | Design a Funhouse Mirror activity where students use their own domain expertise to detect AI distortions, omissions, and overconfidence. Use when students know a subject well enough to evaluate AI claims about it. |
| [[AI Hallucination Fact Check Protocol]] | Design a fact-checking protocol for AI-generated text, extending SIFT with AI-specific adaptations for hallucination detection. Use when students need to verify the factual accuracy of AI claims and citations. For evaluating the reasoning quality of AI output, use ai-output-critical-audit-designer. |
| [[AI Learning Boundary Mapper]] | Map which elements of an assignment benefit from AI assistance vs. which AI use undermines. Use when redesigning tasks for AI-age classrooms or setting defensible AI use policies for specific assignments. |
| [[AI Output Critical Audit Designer]] | Design a structured protocol for auditing AI-generated text against six critical thinking standards. Use when students need to evaluate the reasoning quality of AI output in any subject. For checking factual accuracy and citations, use ai-hallucination-fact-check-protocol. |
| [[AI Socratic Dialogue Designer]] | Design a multi-round questioning sequence for interrogating AI chatbot answers, tracking how responses shift and distinguishing genuine updates from sycophantic capitulation. Use when teaching students to probe AI critically. |
| [[Disciplinary AI Literacy Sequence Designer]] | Design a sequence where students compare AI's handling of the same question across disciplines, developing a mental model of where AI is reliable vs. distorting based on knowledge type. Use when teaching students where AI can and cannot be trusted within and across subject areas. |
| [[Prompt Literacy Sequence Designer]] | Design a learning sequence teaching prompt quality — comparing vague vs. refined prompts to show why specificity and context transform AI output. Use when students use AI without understanding why output quality varies. |
